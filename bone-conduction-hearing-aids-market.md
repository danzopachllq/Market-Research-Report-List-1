<p><p>The bone conduction hearing aids market is categorized into two main types: implantable and non-implantable devices. </p><p>Implantable bone conduction aids, surgically placed under the skin, directly stimulate the cochlea via bone conduction, offering a permanent solution for users with conductive hearing loss or single-sided deafness. </p><p>Non-implantable devices, on the other hand, are worn externally, utilizing vibrations to transmit sound through the skull. They provide a flexible and accessible option for users seeking to improve hearing without surgical intervention.</p></p>
