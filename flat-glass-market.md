<p><p>The flat glass market features several types tailored for various applications. Ordinary flat glass is commonly used for windows and simple structures, characterized by its basic production method. Float glass, produced by floating molten glass on molten tin, ensures higher clarity and uniform thickness, making it ideal for optical applications and high-quality glazing. Rolled glass, formed by rolling molten glass into sheets, is often employed in architectural and decorative uses, offering unique textures and patterns for aesthetic appeal.</p></p>
