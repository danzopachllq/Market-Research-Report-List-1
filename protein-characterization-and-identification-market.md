<p><p>The protein characterization and identification market encompasses techniques such as chromatography, electrophoresis, and mass spectrometry, each serving distinct roles in analyzing proteins. Chromatography separates proteins based on properties like size or charge, enabling purification and analysis. Electrophoresis allows for the separation of proteins in a gel matrix, revealing size and charge differences. Mass spectrometry provides precise molecular weights and structure, offering insights into protein identity and modifications. Together, these methods drive advancements in biochemistry, pharmaceuticals, and clinical diagnostics.</p></p>
