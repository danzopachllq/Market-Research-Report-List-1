<p><p>The tissue sealants market comprises primarily two types: fibrin sealants and synthetic sealants. Fibrin sealants, derived from human plasma, facilitate clotting and wound healing, often used in surgeries to reduce bleeding and promote tissue adhesion. Synthetic sealants, on the other hand, are artificially created substances designed to mimic the properties of natural tissue adhesives, offering advantages like stability and consistency. Both types are essential in medical procedures, enhancing recovery and improving patient outcomes while minimizing complications.</p></p>
