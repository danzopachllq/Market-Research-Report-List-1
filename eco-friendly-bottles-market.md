<p><p>The eco-friendly bottles market comprises various types of sustainable materials, each offering unique benefits. Polylactic Acid (PLA) is derived from renewable resources like corn and is biodegradable. Cellulose, sourced from plants, provides a natural alternative. Starch-based bottles are compostable, while water-soluble polymers dissolve in water. Aliphatic/Aromatic co-polyesters combine flexibility and biodegradability. Moulded fiber, made from recycled paper, is recyclable, while Polyhydroxyalkanoates (PHA) are produced by microorganisms and are backyard compostable. Bio-derived polyethylene substitutes fossil-based plastics with renewable feedstocks, alongside other innovative materials.</p></p>
